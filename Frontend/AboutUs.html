About Map My Blood

A verified blood discovery and direction platform that helps users find nearby blood availability quickly, while enabling hospitals and blood banks to maintain real-time stock data safely and transparently.

About Us In Brief

Map My Blood is a digital support platform built for India to connect blood seekers, verified blood banks, and hospitals. A user enters location, blood group, and urgency level. Our system then filters nearby availability and displays matching results with map-based direction.

The platform is accessible to both public users and medical stakeholders. Separate dashboards ensure role-based access: users search and request information, while blood banks manage stock updates.

How The Platform Works

User Input

Users provide location, required blood type, and urgency status (normal/high/critical).

Database Sorting

Our system fetches nearby blood units by type and priority from verified sources only.

Map + Direction

Results include branch details, distance, contact, and direction to the selected blood source.

Live Inventory Control

Only verified blood banks and hospitals can update their own blood stock in their dashboard.

Registration Criteria

For Users:

  • Valid mobile number and email verification.
  • Basic location permission for nearby search and route assistance.
  • Acceptance of terms, privacy policy, and emergency disclaimer.

For Blood Banks / Hospitals:

  • Government registration certificate and operating license.
  • Authorized representative ID proof and institution verification documents.
  • Branch address validation and emergency contact submission.
  • Approval process by platform verification team before dashboard activation.

Note: Only original and verified blood banks are published in public search results.

Terms and Conditions (Summary)

  • Platform provides location and availability support; final confirmation must be done by direct call.
  • Stock can change rapidly in emergencies; displayed units are based on latest updates by institutions.
  • Any misuse, fake listing, or manipulated stock entry can lead to account suspension.
  • Users must not submit false emergency requests or misuse institution contact numbers.
  • Hospitals and blood banks are solely responsible for accuracy of their own inventory entries.
